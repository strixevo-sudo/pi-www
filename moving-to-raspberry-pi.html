The Pi That's Been Sitting in My Drawer for Months

23 MAR 2026
2 MIN READ
BY STR1X3VO

A few months ago my younger brother handed me a Raspberry Pi 3. I said cheers, put it in my desk drawer, and completely forgot about it. It's been sat there ever since - next to some old cables, a broken mouse and whatever else ends up in that drawer.

That changes now.

// The plan This site is currently running off my main gaming PC inside a Kali Linux VM. The Pi is taking over. Dedicated, low power, always-on hosting - from a board that's been gathering dust for months.

Why it's actually perfect for this

I didn't realise it at the time but a Raspberry Pi 3 is basically ideal for what I need. It pulls around 3 watts under load. It runs Linux natively - no VM needed. It can sit in the corner of my room, plugged in, running Nginx and the Cloudflare Tunnel 24/7 without making a sound or costing anything noticeable on the electric bill.

Right now if I shut my gaming PC down, the site goes offline. That's not great. The Pi fixes that completely - the site stays up whether I'm gaming, sleeping or the PC is off entirely.

What's actually changing

From your end - nothing. Same domain, same URLs, same everything. The only difference is where the server physically lives. Instead of a VM on my gaming rig, it'll be a credit card sized board running the whole thing natively.

There'll be a short window of downtime during the switchover but I'll keep it minimal. Once it's live I'll write up the full setup guide - how I got Nginx, the Cloudflare Tunnel and everything else running on the Pi from scratch.

Cheers for the Pi, bro

Genuinely would not have thought to do this if my brother hadn't just handed it to me. Sometimes the best hardware is the stuff someone else gives you for free.
