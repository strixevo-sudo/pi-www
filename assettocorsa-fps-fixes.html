Why Assetto Corsa Was Running at 47 FPS (And How I Fixed It)

25 MAR 2026
6 MIN READ
BY STR1X3VO

Ryzen 5 5600X. RTX 3050 OC 8GB. 32GB RAM. 1080p. And Assetto Corsa - a game from 2014 - sitting at a rock solid 40 to 47 FPS. Something was wrong.

I'm running the full setup. Custom Shaders Patch preview build by x4fab, Pure by Peter Boese, custom PP filters. The game looks incredible. But it was running like I had a potato in my case instead of a GPU. So I went digging.

The Audit

AC has two config locations that matter - the game folder at steamapps/common/assettocorsa/cfg/ and the user folder at Documents/Assetto Corsa/cfg/. The Documents folder wins. Whatever's in there overrides the game folder. Most people don't know this and end up editing the wrong files.

CSP adds another layer - Documents/Assetto Corsa/cfg/extension/ - where every single CSP module has its own config that overrides the defaults in the game folder. So there's basically three levels of config that all interact with each other.

I went through all of it. Here's what I found.

The Culprits

In rough order of damage done:

// particles_fx.ini - WINDSCREEN LIMIT=100000
One hundred thousand windscreen rain droplet particles. Each one rendered, sorted, and composited every frame. This alone was silently eating frames in the background every single session, rain or shine.
// particles_fx.ini - MARBLES LIMIT=120000
Same story. 120k rubber marble particles left on track. Most tracks don't even generate that many in a full race weekend. The budget was there, the GPU was spending it whether it needed to or not.

Both of those are CSP particle systems that default to sensible values - but somewhere along the way mine had been cranked to absurd levels. Dropped windscreen to 20k and marbles to 50k. Zero visual difference. Instant headroom recovered.

// video.ini - AASAMPLES=8
MSAA 8x. On a 3050. At 1080p. CSP already runs CMAA2 post-process anti-aliasing on top of whatever AC's native AA does - so I was paying 8x MSAA cost for edges that were getting smoothed twice. Dropped to 4x. The difference is invisible once CMAA2 does its pass.
// video.ini - CUBEMAP SIZE=2048 + MIRROR SIZE=2048
Both four times larger than the recommended defaults. The cubemap is what gives car paint its dynamic reflections. 2048 per face, six faces, updating two per frame - expensive. Mirror at 2048 with HQ mode on top. Both dropped to 1024. Still look sharp, fraction of the cost.
// extra_fx.ini - SSGI ENABLED=1, INTENSITY=2, SCALE=1
Screen Space Global Illumination at full resolution with intensity doubled. SSGI tries to fake light bouncing off surfaces back onto other surfaces. One of the heaviest effects CSP offers. Disabled it - HBAO ambient occlusion is still active and covers most of what SSGI was doing visually.
// extra_fx.ini - HBAO RADIUS=2.4, EXP=2.4
HBAO+ ambient occlusion with a very large sampling radius. Double the default values. Pulled them back to sensible levels. Still looks great - just not computing twice as far out as it needs to.

The Bonus Find - A Track Crash

While testing, Shuto Revival Project Beta / Tatsumi PA was crashing instantly on load. The crash trace pointed to INIReader::getFloat failing inside SurfacesManager::loadSurfaceDefinitions. Found it in one line inside tatsumi_pa/data/surfaces.ini:

WAV_PITCH=extended-0

extended-0 is a CSP audio extension syntax. AC's native INI reader hits that value before CSP can intercept it, tries to parse it as a float, and throws. Changed it to 0. Track loads fine now.

Before and After

MSI Afterburner benchmark, same track, same car, same conditions:

BEFORE - Average: 46.4 FPS / Min: 30.2 / Max: 50.0 / 1% low: 32.6
AFTER - Average: 48.6 FPS / Min: 43.2 / Max: 50.0 / 1% low: 42.1

The average didn't move much - but look at the minimums. Before: 30.2 FPS with a 1% low of 32.6. After: 43.2 minimum with a 1% low of 42.1. The floor came up massively. That's what was making the game feel stuttery - not the average FPS, but those drops into the low 30s.

The 50 FPS ceiling is G-Sync Compatible doing its half-rate lock thing. Capped the game at 60 FPS via FPS_CAP_MS=16 in Documents/cfg/video.ini. Locked, smooth, done.

The game still looks exactly the same. Pure is still doing its thing. CSP is still running. The only difference is it's no longer spending GPU time on 100k invisible particles and a full-res global illumination pass on a mid-range GPU.

If you're running CSP + Pure and wondering why your frames are low - go check your Documents configs. Not the game folder. The Documents folder.
