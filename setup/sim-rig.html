The sim rig

The wheel, pedals, and screens I race on — plus the force-feedback and FOV settings that make it feel right.

Hardware

WheelThrustmaster TMX ProAMAZON
Belt-and-gear force feedback. A great entry-level wheel — plenty for Assetto Corsa and iRacing at 1080p.
PedalsThrustmaster T3PA
Three-pedal set — gas, brake, clutch — with adjustable spacing and a conical brake mod for a firmer, more progressive brake feel.
ScreensDual BenQ GW2790 — 27", 1080p, 100Hz IPS. Two screens give a wider field of view across the cockpit, which matters more for racing than raw resolution.

Settings that matter

// Force feedback On the TMX, keep overall FFB high enough to feel the front tyres load up, but pull back in-game gain until clipping stops on the strongest corners. Clipping = lost detail, not more feel.
// FOV Set field of view from your actual seating distance and screen size, not by taste. A correct FOV makes braking points and car placement consistent — most people run it far too wide.

The full reasoning behind the PC side lives on the PC specs page, and the Assetto Corsa graphics tuning is in the FPS write-up.
